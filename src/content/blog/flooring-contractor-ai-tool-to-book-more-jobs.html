Frequently Asked Questions

Will automated messages feel impersonal to my customers?

Only if they're written badly. The goal is to write messages that sound exactly like you — your tone, your sign-off, your business name. Homeowners aren't thinking "is this automated or not?" They're thinking "someone got back to me fast, and they seem professional." That's the impression that matters.

What if I already have an office manager who handles leads?

Automated follow-up works alongside your team, not instead of them. If your office manager is handling leads 9-to-5, the automation covers nights and weekends — the hours where most inquiry volume actually comes from homeowners who are finally off work and researching. Your team still handles the calls and closings.

How quickly can something like this be set up for my business?

A basic follow-up sequence and webchat can typically be configured and running within a few days. The messages are written for your business, connected to your existing phone number or a dedicated line, and tested before going live. There's no months-long implementation or complex tech stack to manage.

What if a lead asks a question the automation can't answer?

The system is designed to capture and qualify, not to replace the sales conversation. If a homeowner asks something specific — like a detailed question about glue-down vs. floating installations — the lead gets flagged and you jump in. Automation handles the response speed problem; your expertise handles the close.
