You just finished a job. Customer seemed happy. Said something like "great work" or "this looks amazing." You packed up your truck and drove off — and that was probably the last time you talked to them.

No review. No follow-up. Nothing.

That's not a customer problem. That's a timing and friction problem. People want to leave reviews — they just don't unless someone makes it ridiculously easy and asks at exactly the right moment.

Here's how to fix that, starting today.

Send the Text Within 2 Hours of Job Completion

This is the single biggest lever you can pull. The moment your tech drives away, the customer's satisfaction is at its peak. They can still smell the fresh caulk, the lawn looks perfect, the AC is blowing cold. That feeling fades fast — within hours, sometimes minutes.

A text at the 90-minute mark hits differently than one sent three days later. Two hours is your window. Don't wait until end of day. Don't send it the next morning. Two hours.

The Templates — Copy These Word for Word

These aren't corporate-approved scripts. They're short, human, and they work. Swap in your name, drop in your Google review link, and send.

Template 1 — Simple and Direct

Hey [First Name], it's [Your Name] from [Company]. Really glad we could get that taken care of for you today. If you have 60 seconds, an honest Google review would mean a lot to us: [LINK]. Thanks!

Template 2 — For Jobs Where the Tech Had a Real Conversation

Hey [First Name] — [Tech Name] said you were awesome to work with today. If the job met your expectations, would you mind dropping us a quick Google review? It genuinely helps. Here's the direct link: [LINK]

Template 3 — Short, Casual, Low Pressure

Thanks for having us out today, [First Name]! If everything looked good, here's our Google page — even a one-liner helps more than you know: [LINK]

Notice what's not in those templates. No begging. No "please please please." No "we'd really appreciate it if you could find the time." Confident, brief, and friendly. That's the tone you want.

What NOT to Say (Seriously)

Don't offer incentives. "Leave us a review and get 10% off your next service" is a Google Terms of Service violation. It can get your listing penalized. Don't do it.

Don't send a wall of text. If they have to scroll, they're not reading it. Keep it under 3 sentences.

Don't be vague. "Let us know how we did" doesn't get reviews. Give them the direct link. Don't make them search for your business on Google — that's three extra steps and you'll lose half of them right there.

Don't ask for a "5-star" review directly. It feels pushy and it's technically against Google's guidelines. Ask for an "honest" review. The good ones will take care of themselves.

What to Do When They Don't Respond

Most people won't respond to the first text. That doesn't mean they're unhappy — it means they got busy. A single follow-up 3–5 days later is completely acceptable and often effective.

Follow-up Template

Hey [First Name], just circling back — hoping everything is still holding up great. If you get a chance, here's that review link: [LINK]. No worries if life got busy!

After two texts, stop. You've done your job. Any more and it starts to feel like harassment, which will hurt you more than a missing review ever would.

Quick FAQ

What if the customer had a bad experience?
Your text should only go out if the job was closed as completed without issues. Build a simple logic check into your workflow — if there's an open complaint or callback, suppress the review request until it's resolved.

Should I use text or email?
Text. Open rates for SMS are around 98%. Email open rates hover around 20%. For follow-ups this time-sensitive, there's no contest.

How do I get my Google review link?
Go to your Google Business Profile, click "Share profile," and copy the review link. Paste it directly into your templates.
